Bridging Quality and Energy Efficiency with Spiking Mamba for Speech Bandwidth Extension

Donghyun Kim, Sangho Han and Joon-Hyuk Chang †
Department of Electronic Engineering
Hanyang University, Seoul, Republic of Korea
{utahboy3502, vxgkstkdhgxx, jchang}@hanyang.ac.kr
Abstract

Artificial neural networks have significantly advanced speech bandwidth extension (BWE) but suffer from high computational complexity, limiting deployment on power-constrained edge devices. While spiking neural networks (SNNs) offer an energy-efficient alternative, they often struggle to capture the long-range temporal dependencies required for high-fidelity reconstruction. To address this, we propose SpikeBWE+ to synergize SNN efficiency with the global modeling capabilities of the Mamba architecture. Experimental evaluations on the achieves reconstruction quality comparable to state-of-the-art baselines while reducing energy consumption by an order or magnitude, establishing a new benchmark for efficient, real-time speech restoration.
